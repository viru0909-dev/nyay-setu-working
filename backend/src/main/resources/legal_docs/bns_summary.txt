# Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) & Procedural Guide for Nyay-Setu Vakil-Friend

## General Advice
When a user describes an incident, analyze it according to the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023, which replaced the Indian Penal Code (IPC).
If a user is asking about filing a case for a serious crime (e.g., murder, rape, theft, assault), they MUST file a First Information Report (FIR) with the POLICE first, not the CIVIL COURT.
For property disputes, divorces, or breach of contract, they should file with the COURT.

## Common Offenses under BNS 2023

1. **Theft (Chori)**
   - BNS Section 303 (formerly IPC 379)
   - Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment, or fine, or both.
   - For theft in a dwelling house, Section 305 applies (up to 7 years).

2. **Snatching**
   - BNS Section 304 (New specific offense)
   - Theft is "snatching" if the offender suddenly or quickly or forcibly seizes or secures or grabs or takes away from any person or from his possession any movable property.
   - Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment, and fine.

3. **Cheating (Dhokhadhadi) & Fraud**
   - BNS Section 318 (formerly IPC 420)
   - Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property.
   - Punishment: Up to 7 years imprisonment, and shall also be liable to fine.

4. **Criminal Intimidation (Threatening)**
   - BNS Section 351 (formerly IPC 506)
   - Threatening another with injury to person, reputation, or property.
   - Punishment: Up to 2 years, or fine, or both. If threat is to cause death or grievous hurt: up to 7 years.

5. **Assault or Criminal Force**
   - BNS Section 131 / 132 (formerly IPC 351/352)
   - Using criminal force otherwise than on grave provocation.
   - Punishment: Up to 3 months, or fine up to 500 rupees, or both.

6. **Rape and Sexual Offenses**
   - BNS Section 63 (formerly IPC 376)
   - Rape.
   - Punishment: Rigorous imprisonment not less than 10 years, which may extend to life imprisonment, and fine.

7. **Murder (Hatya)**
   - BNS Section 103 (formerly IPC 302)
   - Punishment: Death or imprisonment for life, and fine.

8. **Hit and Run**
   - BNS Section 106 (2)
   - Causing death by rash and negligent driving and escaping without reporting to police.
   - Punishment: Up to 10 years imprisonment and fine. (Note: Non-reporting provision is currently under review, but rash driving causing death is strictly punishable).

## Civil Cases & Family Law
- **Divorce / Family Disputes:** Handled under Hindu Marriage Act, Special Marriage Act, etc. (Not BNS). Target is COURT. Case Type is FAMILY.
- **Property Disputes:** Handled under Transfer of Property Act, Specific Relief Act. Target is COURT. Case Type is PROPERTY.
- **Contract Breach:** Target is COURT. Case Type is COMMERCIAL or CIVIL.

## Filing Urgency Guidelines
- CRITICAL: Ongoing physical threat, murder, kidnapping, sexual assault.
- URGENT: Recent theft, escalating domestic violence, imminent property destruction.
- NORMAL: Past fraud, civil disputes, divorce filing, old property disagreements.

## AI Instructions
Always refer to the user's situation empathetically. If they describe a BNS offense, mention the relevant BNS Section (e.g., "Under Section 303 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita..."). Recommend whether they need to file an FIR (Police) or a civil suit (Court).
